Phase 1

This will give you your starting position.

Directions

Right > Down > Right > Up > (repeat)

Strategy

Start at column 1, row 1. Scan in the current direction until you reach the next letter in sequence. Once you reach the letter, change to the next direction and seek the next letter.

Example

Using the example "amazon" 
			Scan 	right 	to 	A
			Scan 	down 	to 	M
			Scan 	right 	to 	A
			Scan 	up 	to 	Z
			Scan 	right 	to 	O
			Scan 	down 	to 	N

Phase 2

This will allow you to retrieve your password (following phase 1)

Directions

Left > Up > Left > Down > Right > Down > (repeat)

Strategy

Scan in the current direction until you reach the next letter in sequence. Once you reach the letter, "overshoot" by two characters and write those characters down. From the position you end up in, change to the next direction and seek the next character in squence.

Once you have found each of the sets of two characters, combine them in to one long string to get your password!

What if?

If the last character of "overshoot" characters happens to be the next character you are seeking, simply move one further square in the direction you are currently moving before switching direction and seeking the next character.

If you are seeking in a direction and you reach the end of the grid, just wrap around and continue in the same direction from the other end of the grid.

Example

Using the example "amazon" 
			Scan 	left 	to	A	take next two characters (e.g.) Fe
			Scan 	up 	to	M	take next two characters (e.g.) vf
			Scan 	left 	to	A	take next two characters (e.g.) Gw
			Scan 	down 	to	Z	take next two characters (e.g.) zO
			*Note: we finish on O, so move down one more row before seeking right to O
			Scan 	right 	to	O	take next two characters (e.g.) QG
			Scan 	down 	to	N	take next two characters (e.g.) kP
Combined in to one long string: FevfGwzOQGkP

Extras

Padding

If you have used the extra option for padding, during phase 2, in addition to the two characters you write down, you should also write down the non-alphabetic character that appears in the outer border of the grid (by continuing in the same direction)

Example

Using the example "amazon" 
			Scan 	left 	to	A	take next two characters (e.g.) Fe	and outer character	]
			Scan 	up 	to	M	take next two characters (e.g.) vf	and outer character	4
			Scan 	left 	to	A	take next two characters (e.g.) Gw	and outer character	8
			Scan 	down 	to	Z	take next two characters (e.g.) nG	and outer character	+
			Scan 	right 	to	O	take next two characters (e.g.) QZ	and outer character	2
			Scan 	down 	to	N	take next two characters (e.g.) kP	and outer character	;
Combined in to one long string: FevfGwnGQZkP]48+2;
